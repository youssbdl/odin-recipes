Ramen

Description

Ramen is a popular Japanese noodle soup dish consisting of Chinese-style wheat noodles served in a rich broth.It's enjoyed for its comforting, savory taste and is popular both in restaurants and as instant noodles worldwide.

Inredients

    For the broth
  • Chicken or pork broth: 4 cups (homemade or store-bought)
  • Water 2 cups:
  • Soy sauce: 2 tablespoons
  • Miso paste: 1 teaspoon (optional for richness)
  • Mirin (sweet rice wine) or sugar: 1 teaspoon
  • Kombu (dried kelp): 1 piece
  • Ginger: 1 inch piece, sliced
  • Garlic: 3 cloves, smashed
      • For the Noodles:
  • Ramen noodles: 8oz, (fresh or dried)
      • For the Toppings:
  • Eggs: 2, soft-boiled (boiled for 6-7 minutes)
  • Pork(chashu),chicken or tofu: 1 cup, cooked and sliced
  • Green onions: 1/2 cup, sliced
  • Nori(seaweed): 1/2 cup, cut into strips
  • Bean sprouts: 1/2 cup
  • Sesame seeds (optional)
  • Bok choy or spinach (optional)

    • Steps

    1. Prepare the broth:
      2.
      • In a large pot, combine chicken or pork broth, water, soy sauce, miso paste, mirin, kombu, ginger, and garlic
      • Bring to a simmer over medium heat and cook for about 20-30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld. Remove the kombu after about 10 minutes (if used)
      • Strain the broth to remove the solids and keep it warm on low heat
    2. Cook the Noodles:
      3.
      • In a separate pot, bring water to a boil. Cook the ramen noodles according to package instructions (usually 3-5 minutes for fresh noodles or 5-7 minutes for dried)
      • Drain the noodles and rinse them briefly under cold water to stop the cooking process
    3. Assemble the Ramen:
      4.
      • Divide the cooked noodles among bowls
      • Ladle the hot broth over the noodles
      • Top with sliced pork (or chicken/tofu), halved soft-boiled eggs, green onions, nori strips, bean sprouts, and any additional toppings you like
      • Sprinkle with sesame seeds if desired

      Tips

      • Feel free to customize the toppings based on your preferences. Other options include corn, mushrooms, or bamboo shoots
      • For extra flavor, you can saut√© some garlic and ginger before adding the broth

      Enjoy!
