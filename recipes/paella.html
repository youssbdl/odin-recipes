Paella

Description

Paella is a traditional Spanish dish originating from the region of Valencia, typically cooked in a wide, shallow pan over an open flame. It's a rice-based dish that often incorporates a combination of meats, seafood, vegetables, and saffron, which gives it its characteristic golden color and distinctive flavor. Paella is considered one of Spain's most iconic meals and can vary greatly depending on local ingredients and preferences.

Inredients

    Basic Ingredients
  • Olive oil: 2 tablespoons
  • Rice: 1 ½ cups (300g) bomba or arborio rice (short-grain)
  • Saffron: A pinch of saffron threads (soaked in ¼ cup warm water)
  • Smoked Paprika: 1 teaspoon
  • Chicken broth: 4 cups (1 liter) (or seafood/fish broth)
      • Proteins
  • Chicken: ½ lb (225g) chicken thighs or drumsticks, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • Spanish chorizo: ½ lb (225g), sliced
  • Shrimp: ½ lb (225g), peeled and deveined
  • Mussels or clams ½ lb (225g), cleaned
      • Vegetables
  • Onion: 1, finely chopped
  • Garlic: 3 cloves, minced
  • Red bell pepper: 1, chopped
  • Green bell pepper: 1, chopped
  • Tomato: 1 ripe tomato, grated or chopped finely
  • Frozen peas: 1 cup (150g)
      • Addition Ingredients
  • White whine: ½ cup (120ml), dry
  • Salt and pepper: To taste
  • Fresh parsley: Chopped, for garnish
  • Lemon wedges: For serving

    • These ingredients can be adapted depending on the type of paella you want to make (Valenciana, seafood, or mixed).

    Steps

    1. Sauté Ingredients:
      2.
      • Heat the olive oil in a large paella pan or a wide skillet over medium heat
      • Add the chicken pieces, season with salt and pepper, and brown them for 5-7 minutes. Remove from the pan and set aside
      • In the same pan, sauté the chorizo slices until they release their flavor. Remove and set aside
      • Add the onion and garlic to the pan, cook until softened (about 3 minutes), then stir in the bell peppers. Cook for another 5 minutes
    2. Add Tomato and Spices:
      3.
      • Stir in the grated or chopped tomato and cook until the mixture reduces a bit (about 3 minutes)
      • Add the smoked paprika and the soaked saffron (with the water), mixing well
    3. Toast the Rice:
      4.
      • Stir in the rice and let it cook for 2 minutes, allowing it to soak up all the flavors
    4. Deglaze with Wine:
      5.
      • Pour in the white wine and let it simmer until the alcohol evaporates (about 2 minutes)
    5. Add Broth and Cook:
      6.
      • Pour in the chicken broth and return the chicken and chorizo to the pan. Stir to combine
      • Simmer the mixture over medium heat for about 15-18 minutes without stirring, letting the rice absorb the liquid. Add a bit of extra broth or water if needed
    6. Seafood and Final Touch:
      7.
      • Nestle the shrimp, mussels, and peas into the paella. Cover the pan with a lid for another 10 minutes
    7. Finish and Serve:
      8.
      • Let the paella rest for 5 minutes off the heat
      • Garnish with fresh parsley and serve with lemon wedges on the side

    Enjoy!
